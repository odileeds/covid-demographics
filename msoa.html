About these maps

This visualisation was created by Open Innovations and uses hex cartograms. A hex cartogram gives each area the same visual weight on the map at the expense of accurate geography. This helps make geographically compact (but often big in population) areas more easily visible than a geographic map. Using consistent shapes (hexagons) also makes it easier to spot outliers.

The travel time values are calculated by finding the minimum travel time by each mode to an MSOA with a vaccination site from the current MSOA. Times in the source data are limited to 60 minutes.

Data sources

  • Cartogram: created by the House of Commons Library (Open Parliament Licence).
  • MSOA names: the MSOA names come from the House of Commons Library.
  • Demographic data: obtained from www.localhealth.org.uk, Public Health England. Contains public sector information licensed under the Open Government Licence v3.0 via a CSV extract of Local Health
  • Testing data: obtained from PHE testing data (Open Government Licence v3.0)
  • Vaccination data: obtained from NHS England (Open Government Licence v3.0) via extracted MSOA data.
  • Vaccination sites: vaccinations sites listed by NHS England (Open Government Licence v3.0) via processed CSV file of sites.
  • Travel time data: created by Tom Forth & Open Innovations (CC-BY 4.0) using data from public transport timetables (Open Government Licence v3.0) and OpenStreetMap (ODbL).

To power the visualisation we compile all the data by MSOA in a single CSV file.
